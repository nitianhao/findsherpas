Summary

Search risk at a glance

The short version

We tested 25 realistic customer queries against Huckberry's search, live on site. The audit checks whether customers see a relevant top result, not just whether the search engine returns something.

This report focuses on the 11 queries where a customer likely hits a dead end: an off-topic result, a missing interpretation, a dropped constraint, or a relevant product buried too far down.

The single highest-ROI fix to start with: Fix relevance score ranking algorithm.

Recommendations

Prioritized Roadmap

1

Fix relevance score ranking algorithm

Addresses poor ranking across Product Discovery, Language Understanding, Brand Search, and Shopping Context by ensuring highest-scoring results appear first.

Critical Quick Win
2

Implement category mapping for common queries

Fixes the "Sale" query failure in Product Discovery by recognizing category-intent queries and filtering to appropriate product sets.

Critical Medium Effort
3

Add brand name fuzzy matching and compound word splitting

Resolves Typo Tolerance failures for "Portugese Flannel" and "flintandtinder" by recognizing misspelled and merged brand names.

Critical Medium Effort
4

Enforce brand constraints as mandatory filters

Fixes Brand & Model Search and Filters & Constraints by ensuring brand names in queries act as required filters, not optional keywords.

Critical Quick Win
5

Implement semantic understanding for occasion-based queries

Addresses Shopping Context failures like "office casual shorts" by mapping lifestyle terms to appropriate product categories.

Critical Major Project
6

Add product type prioritization for multi-term queries

Fixes Language Understanding issues like "cargo pants short" by boosting exact product type matches over partial matches.

High Medium Effort
7

Improve special character handling in measurements

Resolves Typo Tolerance issues with queries like "shorts 7"" by better parsing measurement specifications.

Medium Quick Win
