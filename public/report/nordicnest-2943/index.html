Summary

Search risk at a glance

The short version

We tested 57 realistic customer queries against Nordic Nest's search, live on site. The audit checks whether customers see a relevant top result, not just whether the search engine returns something.

This report focuses on the 31 queries where a customer likely hits a dead end: an off-topic result, a missing interpretation, a dropped constraint, or a relevant product buried too far down.

The first fix to ship: Implement brand priority boosting for exact brand matches — Fixes Product Discovery and Brand & Model Search by ensuring brand-specific queries like "fiskars" and "staub gryta" show only that brand's products first.

Advanced Diagnostics

What the visible result set reveals

These checks stay inside the same query → results → audit boundary, but look beyond the single best result.

Result Set Purity

This checks how much of the visible result set is actually relevant, not just whether one good result exists somewhere. A noisy top 5 means customers must visually filter around weak results even when search technically found a match.

35%
top 5 relevant

Result

The visible result set is noisy: only 35% of top 5 results were relevant on average, and top 10 purity was 29%.

Evidence

  • "fiskars": 0/5 relevant in top 5
  • "textil": 0/5 relevant in top 5
  • "prylar till köket": 0/5 relevant in top 5
  • "grejer för utomhus": 0/5 relevant in top 5

How to remedy it

Tune ranking to demote low-relevance partial matches once a strong intent match exists. Add a top-N quality guardrail: for each important query class, require a minimum share of relevant results in the top 5, not only a good best-result position.

Top Result Trust

This asks whether the first result is defensible as the best thing to show a customer. A bad #1 is the clearest search failure because it shapes the shopper's first impression.

68%
questionable #1 results

Result

The first result is not reliable enough: 39 of 57 queries (68%) had a weak, partial, or clearly beaten #1 result.

Evidence

  • "kan ni rekommendera bra kastruller": #1 was "No results" (score 0.00). No visible result was available.
  • "stor tefal stekpanna": #1 was "No results" (score 0.00). No visible result was available.
  • "12 tums stekpanna": #1 was "No results" (score 0.00). No visible result was available.
  • "kastrull under 500 kr": #1 was "No results" (score 0.00). No visible result was available.

How to remedy it

Add a stricter first-result threshold for high-intent queries. If the top result is weak and a stronger result exists lower, boost the stronger exact/category/brand match or suppress the weak partial match from rank #1.

Attribute Drift

This decomposes multi-attribute queries to see which terms survive into the top results. It distinguishes right-category-but-wrong-attribute failures from total search failures.

8
queries with attribute loss

Result

8 multi-attribute queries showed some attribute loss in the top 5. The audit separates preserved, partially preserved, and dropped terms so the team can see which part of the query is being lost.

Evidence

  • "stor tefal stekpanna": dropped stor, tefal, stekpanna; partially preserved none
  • "12 tums stekpanna": dropped 12, tums, stekpanna; partially preserved none
  • "kastrull under 500 kr": dropped kastrull, 500, kr; partially preserved none
  • "billig stekpanna": dropped billig, stekpanna; partially preserved none

How to remedy it

Extract query attributes before ranking and give required attributes explicit weight. For brand, size, material, activity, and style terms, penalize results that match the product type but omit or contradict the attribute.

Diversity vs Redundancy

This checks whether broad or exploratory queries show useful choice, or whether the top results are flooded by repeated variants. It is most useful for multi-brand or broad-catalog ecommerce, and less useful for true single-brand stores.

8
unique families in worst broad top 10

Result

The most redundant broad result set was "kastrull": 8 unique product families across 10 top results.

Evidence

  • "kastrull": 8 unique product families across 10 top results
  • "kastruller": 8 unique product families across 10 top results
  • "belysning": 10 unique product families across 10 top results
  • "ljusstake": 10 unique product families across 10 top results

How to remedy it

Group color/size/variant duplicates under a product-family cap for broad queries. Keep exact product searches variant-rich, but diversify broad discovery queries across families, brands, styles, and use cases.

#1 kan ni rekommendera bra kastruller Zero results

The search returned zero results — the customer reached a dead end.

Evidence

The search engine returned zero results for the Swedish query 'kan ni rekommendera bra kastruller' (can you recommend good pots/pans). Despite having a best result at position #16 according to the stats, the customer saw no results at all, indicating a complete failure to surface any cookware products.

Fix

Add query rewriting, token splitting, and fallback matching for this query pattern so customers do not hit a dead end.

#2 stor tefal stekpanna No fuzzy matching

The search returned zero results — the customer reached a dead end.

Evidence

Query 'stor tefal stekpanna' returned zero results despite containing a clear misspelling of 'stor' (should be 'stor' meaning 'large' in Swedish) and 'stekpanna' (Swedish for 'frying pan'). The engine failed to recognize that this is a search for large Tefal frying pans and provided no fuzzy matching or spelling correction.

Fix

Add typo and fuzzy matching for high-value product, category, and brand terms so small spelling errors still reach the right products.

#3 12 tums stekpanna Facet not extracted

The search returned zero results — the customer reached a dead end.

Evidence

The engine failed to recognize '12 tums' as a size filter and 'stekpanna' as a product category, treating them only as literal keywords to match in titles.

Fix

Extract this as a structured facet and use it as a ranking/filtering signal instead of treating it as loose text.
